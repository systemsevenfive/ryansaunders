Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 10, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Kitchen Synk handles Your information when You use the Application.

Kitchen Synk does not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data. Your timer data lives on Your device. If You choose to enable iCloud, that data syncs across Your own devices through Apple's CloudKit service. The developer never sees it.

Interpretation and Definitions

Interpretation

The words of which the initial letter is capitalized have meanings defined under the following conditions. The following definitions shall have the same meaning regardless of whether they appear in singular or in plural.

Definitions

For the purposes of this Privacy Policy:

  • Application refers to Kitchen Synk, the software program provided by the Developer.
  • Developer (referred to as either "the Developer", "We", "Us" or "Our" in this Policy) refers to Ryan Saunders, an independent developer based in Ontario, Canada.
  • Country refers to: Ontario, Canada.
  • Device means any device that can run the Application, such as an iPhone, iPad, or Mac.
  • Personal Data is any information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual.
  • Service refers to the Application.
  • You means the individual using the Application.

Data We Do Not Collect

Kitchen Synk does not collect Personal Data. Specifically, the Application does not collect or transmit:

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or any other identifier.
  • Your IP address, device identifiers, advertising identifiers, or location.
  • Usage analytics, crash telemetry, or behavioural data.
  • The names, durations, or contents of Your timers.

There are no user accounts. There are no in-app purchases. There are no third-party SDKs, no advertising networks, and no analytics services embedded in the Application. The Apple Privacy Nutrition Label submitted with this app declares zero data collection.

Data Stored on Your Device

Kitchen Synk uses SwiftData to store Your timers, presets, and history locally on Your Device. A small amount of preference information is stored in the system UserDefaults store (for example, Your selected display options). All of this data stays on Your Device unless You enable iCloud sync.

iCloud Sync

If You are signed into iCloud and have iCloud Drive enabled for Kitchen Synk, Your timer data is synchronised across Your own devices using Apple's CloudKit service. This sync is end-to-end between Your devices through Your iCloud account. The Developer does not operate any servers, does not have access to Your iCloud container, and cannot read or modify Your timer data.

iCloud sync is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy. You can disable sync at any time in the Settings app, under Your Apple Account, iCloud, Apps Using iCloud, Kitchen Synk.

Notifications

If You grant notification permission, Kitchen Synk schedules local notifications on Your Device so that Your timers can alert You when they finish, even if the Application is not in the foreground. These notifications are scheduled and delivered locally by the operating system. No notification content is sent through the Developer's servers, because the Developer does not operate any.

Apple Intelligence and On-Device Suggestions

Kitchen Synk uses Apple's on-device FoundationModels framework to suggest cooking durations based on the name and details of a timer You are creating. This processing happens entirely on Your Device. The text You enter into the Application is not transmitted off the Device for this purpose, and is not shared with the Developer or any third party.

Background Activity

Kitchen Synk uses the system Background Tasks framework to keep Your active timers accurate while the Application is suspended. This activity occurs locally on the Device and does not involve any network communication initiated by the Application.

Third Parties

Kitchen Synk does not share data with third parties. There are no advertising networks, analytics providers, marketing partners, affiliates, or service providers handling Your data on the Developer's behalf. The only third party involved in the operation of the Application is Apple, in its capacity as the operator of the iCloud and CloudKit infrastructure that You may optionally use to sync Your own data between Your own devices.

Deleting Your Data

Because all timer data is stored on Your Device or in Your own iCloud account, You are in control of it.

  • To delete the data on a single Device, delete the Application from that Device.
  • To delete the data synced through iCloud, open the Settings app, navigate to Your Apple Account, iCloud, Manage Account Storage, Kitchen Synk, and choose Delete Data.

The Developer cannot delete data on Your behalf, because the Developer does not have access to it.

Children's Privacy

Kitchen Synk is suitable for general audiences and does not collect data from anyone, including children. Because no Personal Data is collected, no data is collected from anyone under the age of 13.

Security

Data stored on Your Device is protected by the standard iOS, iPadOS, and macOS security model, including device encryption when Your Device is locked with a passcode. Data synced through iCloud is protected in transit and at rest by Apple's CloudKit infrastructure. No method of electronic storage is 100% secure, but the Application does not introduce any additional transmission paths beyond those provided by Apple.

Links to Other Websites

The Application and its supporting website may contain links to websites that are not operated by the Developer. The Developer has no control over and assumes no responsibility for the content or privacy practices of any third party sites.

Changes to this Privacy Policy

The Developer may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page and the "Last updated" date at the top will be revised. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically.

Contact

Questions about this Privacy Policy can be sent through the Kitchen Synk contact form.
